Today’s Briefing — 2026-07-08

Analysis

Two weeks out from the 23 July MPC and the honest read is little changed since yesterday. The SARB board still carries the 3 July close verbatim — repo 7.00%, prime 10.50%, ZAR 16.21/$, R2030 7.675%, R209 8.395%, NEER 86.18 — so nothing domestic has re-rated. Gold is essentially flat at $4,127/oz, platinum unchanged at $1,649, and Brent is steady near $72.9 after OPEC+ agreed another supply increase for next month, which keeps the imported-inflation channel that justified May's hike dormant. The JSE All Share prints ~111,164 on the 8 July tape, unchanged from Monday's close — range-bound, not trending — while the rand ticked marginally softer to ~16.26/$ on the Trading Economics tape but holds comfortably below 17.00. The one fresh wrinkle worth logging: Governor Kganyago has reiterated that further hikes remain possible and flagged higher fertiliser costs feeding into H2 food prices — a supply-side risk to monitor, but not enough to shift the base case for a hold.

Forecast check by 2026-07-31

Supplementary: the rand ends July stronger than 17.00/$.

Policy desk

OPEC+, led by Saudi Arabia, agreeing another production-quota increase for next month caps the oil-shock channel that drove May's hawkish framing, so the SARB should hold at 7.00% on 23 July and retire the oil-shock language. But it should stop short of pre-committing to cuts: Kganyago's warning that oil won't quickly return to pre-conflict levels and that higher fertiliser costs could spill into H2 food prices is a live supply-side risk, so the statement should keep optionality and let the June/July CPI prints do the talking. On the fiscal side, with growth pinned at 1.2% for 2026, Treasury should press logistics and electricity reform to lift the growth floor rather than lean on the policy rate, and finally legislate the rules-based fuel-levy stabilisation band this ledger has flagged since 3 July so the next oil swing is absorbed fiscally.
